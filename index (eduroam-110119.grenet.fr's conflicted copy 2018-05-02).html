Co-Regulation (CORE) Lab.

We study co-regulation in romantic relationships. We study social thermoregulation. We rely on open science ideals. We collaborate with researchers around the world, but are located at the Université Grenoble Alpes.


Papers

Check out publications and/or preprints from the lab. We try to ensure all our work is available to the public, so if you can't find a paper let us know!

Open Data/Materials

Projects

Here’s a sample of the work we do.

Human Penguin Project

International collaboration investigating the link between social environment and core body temperature.

Socially Thermoregulated Thinking

Past experiences, and a current manipulation of physical temperature, affect thinking about our loved ones.

Machine Learning and the Stroop Effect

Revisiting what predicts performance on a classic effect using new statistics (conditional random forests).

Many Labs 1

36 labs replicated 13 classic psychology studies to investigate cultural and contextual predictors of replicability.

Transparency Guide

One of our researchers participated in a collaboration to write a practical guide for more transparent science.

Oxford Bibliography Guide

Two of our researchers participated in a collaboration to write an Oxford Bibliography on replication initiatives in psychology.

Replication Attachment-Social Value Orientation

One of our researchers conducted a (failed) replication of a famous finding in the literature (showing a relation between attachment security and Social Value Orientation).

Opportunities/Openings

We sometimes have job openings at the lab. In the meantime, if you are interested in working with us, there are various grants you can receive and where we can support you in writing. We have posted some of these below and will update as we learn about more. We work in English, but we also invite French (or other) speakers who want to improve their English.

Internships

We welcome interns from fields like psychology, computer science, statistics, or others. We currently have several students scheduled for 8-week visits.

Postdoc Grant - Marie Curie

The Marie Curie grant is an excellent opportunity for scholars outside of France wanting to come to France. Topic is flexible. Deadline for this call is September 12, 2018

Postdoc Grant - Prestige

The Prestige grant is an excellent opportunity, but needs cofinancing from another source. Deadlines throughout the year

Postdoc Grant - International Agency for Research on Cancer

In our papers, we have written about links between cancer and thermoregulation. There are good reasons to suppose a link. This grant could support such research. Currently closed, but may reopen

Postdoc Grant - Fondation Fyssen

The Prestige grant is an excellent opportunity, but needs cofinancing from another source. Only open to researchers outside of France. Deadline closed for this year

Make Our Planet Great Again

Want to study thermoregulation and climate change? The French government has heavily invested in research countering climate change. Different grants available

China Scholarship Council

From China and want to come work with us in France? The CSC has various grants available for Chinese scholars

Fullbright Scholarships

US Scholar and want to come work with us in France (if only for a few months)? Fullbright has various opportunities at various level of your career

Brazilian Scholarships

Brazilian Scholar and want to come work with us in France (if only for a few months)? There are various scholarships available (and we speak some Portuguese too!)

Software Grants

We are working on developing tech (deep learning, app development. Various grants are available for this. The Catalyst grant is but one example. Get in touch to see how we can cooperate!

IDEX Grants

The Initiative d’Excellence (of which UGA is a part) offers opportunities for various strategic partners (including in Brazil, US, Canada, Japan, Singapore, Mexico, Senegal, Taiwan, and Morocco. Get in touch to explore with us

National Cancer Institute Exploratory Grant

In our papers, we have written about links between cancer and thermoregulation. There are good reasons to suppose a link. This grant could support such research.

Get in touch

If you’re interested in a position in the lab, contact us! You can work on co-regulation or social thermoregulation in combination with your own topic (e.g., computational modelling, motivation, et cetera). In your contact request, please include why you are interested in working on concepts related to thermoregulation/co-regulation. Please also include:
  • Which opportunity (e.g., grant) you’re interested in.
  • Current academic/professional status.
  • A brief statement about your commitment to open science.
